Why tensor-channel visuals were demoted
- Tensor channels are analytically useful for model diagnostics, but they are not basketball-readable for stakeholder communication.
- Court-based plots using actual shot locations make subtype structure, player style, and drift much easier to interpret in basketball terms.

Remaining limitations
- Subtype aggregate court plots use all matched season-level shot rows inside each subtype, so they communicate shot-location tendencies but not explicit make-rate surfaces.
- Drift case studies are selected automatically from available players and may not be the single best storytelling examples without manual curation.
- Dossier panels are compact by design and therefore summarize only the most important text fields.
